Main Facts:

  • Michael Keaton as Betelgeuse (pronounced Beetlejuice), a ghoul and freelancing bio-exorcist.
  • Betelgeuse only needs to be exhumed from his grave to be summoned, after which he is free to wreak havoc; he cannot be summoned or controlled by saying his name three times, and wanders the world freely, appearing to torment different characters in different manifestations.
  • If you say his name three times, HE APPEARS!
  • Beetlejuice had an animated cartoon series from 1989-1991, which revolved around the adventures he went on with his pal Lydia.
  • The movie itself was a critical and commercial success, grossing $74.2 million from a budget of $15 million.
  • There are plenty of false rumors about a sequel to Beetlejuice, sorry, it ain't happening.


    Interesting Facts:

  • Beetlejuice is not DEAD
  • Beetlejuice doesn't actually appear until 25 minutes into the film and actually appears in less than 20 percent of the film in its entirety, 17.5 minutes. Keaton only spent two weeks filming.
  • The character is named for a bright red star in the constellation of Orion, Betelgeuse.
  • The original script for Beetlejuice was much more dark and sinister. It was going to include a lot of gruesome violence.
  • Betelgeuse/Beetlejuice's name is spoken 15 times in the film. Eight from Barbara, two from Juno, and five from Lydia. Adam also says his name but mispronounces it.
  • Burton left much of Beetlejuice's character development up to Keaton, simply telling him that the character "lived in every time period, but was of no time period."
